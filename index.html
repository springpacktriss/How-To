HTML Coding

Note: for this first "how to" subject, I can not simply put the code on the page, or the computer will think that it is part of the HTML coding that you can not see unless you inspect the page. So, if there is an asterick (*), then you do not need the dots that are in the code that I am showing to you.

For coding you need...

  • An html tag, like this: *<.html> <./html>

  • A head tag, like this: *<.head> <./head>

  • A body tag, like this: *<.body> <./body>

  • A title tag, like this: *<.title> <./title>

  • Different size tags, like these: *<.h1> <./h1>, *<.h2> <./h2>, etc. Note: this only goes up to six, and "h1" is the biggest, and "h6" is the smallest.

  • list tags, like the ones that I am using like this: *<.ul> <./ul>, *<.ol> <./ol>, *<.li> <./li>

Breakfast Ideas

Eggs and Toast

This simple yet delisious meal is easy to make. To make it, you will need...

  1. Eggs

  2. Toast

  3. Butter (optional)

  4. Salt and pepper (optional)

  5. Pan spray

  6. Frying pan

  7. Toaster

  8. Small bowl (optional)

  9. Metal spatula

  10. Plate

Directions

  • First, heat the pan on medium and spray with pan spray.

  • Then, crack the eggs on the side of the pan or the side of a smal bowl.

  • While the eggs are cooking, put some toast in the toaster and toast to your desire. Add butter if you would like.

  • When the whites aroun the eggs are solid, put salt and pepper on them if you would like, and then flip them.

  • When the eggs are done to your liking, use the metal spatula to pick the eggs off of the pan and on to a plate.

  • Sit down and enjoy!

Steak, Hashbrowns, and Eggs

This was the only picture that I could find that worked.

This meal is a little more work, but it is absolutely delicious. To make it, you will need...

  1. Steak

  2. Hashbrowns (you can use hashbrowns from a bag or make your own.)

  3. Eggs

  4. Butter (optional)

  5. Salt and pepper (optional)

  6. Pan spray

  7. Frying pan

  8. Toaster (it goes along with the toast.)

  9. Small bowl (optional)

  10. Metal spatula

  11. Plate

  12. Toast

Directions

  • First, heat the pan on medium and spray with pan spray.

  • Then, put the steak in the pan and cook to your desire. When the steak is done, use the metal spatula to take it off the pan and on to a plate.

  • Next, put the hashbrowns in the pan and cook to your desire.

  • While the hashbrowns are cooking, put some toast in the toaster and toast to desired texture. Add butter if you would like.

  • When the hashbrowns are done, use the metal spatula to take them off the pan and on to the plate.

  • Next, crack the eggs on the side of the pan or the side of a smal bowl.

  • When the whites aroun the eggs are solid, put salt and pepper on them if you would like, and then flip them.

  • When the eggs are done to your liking, use the metal spatula to pick the eggs off of the pan and on to the plate.

  • Sit down and enjoy!

Note: You can customize the ingredients and cooking times to suit your preferences in any of these recipes, and feel welcome to increase the amount of food being made if you are making this for more than one person.

Lunch ideas

Chicken Salad

This is a very customizable dish of flavor and healthy satisfaction. You can add just about anything you want, including fruit! To make this meal, you will need...

  1. Chicken (you can use canned chicken or cook your own.)

  2. Salt and pepper (optional)

  3. Any other add-ins that you would like, such as grapes, apples, nuts, tomatoes, cucumbers, etc.

  4. Plate

  5. Sauces, such as ranch or hot sauce.

  6. Lettuce, any other greenery that you would like to add in.

Directions

  • First, if you are using canned chicken, drain the liquid and put the chicken in a bowl. If you are cooking your own chicken, cook it to your desire and then cut it up and put it in a bowl.

  • Then, add salt and pepper if you would like.

  • Next, put in the greenery.

  • After that, add any other add-ins that you would like and mix together.

  • Finally,put a serving of the chicken salad on a plate with any sauces that you would like and enjoy!

Hot Dogs

This is a classic bonfire meal, but you can cook it in your house or on the grill as well! It has several customizable options. To make it, you will need...

  • Hot dogs

  • Hot dog buns

  • Condiments, such as ketchup, mustard, relish, etc.

  • Plate

  • Grill, or stove, or bonfire, or microwave

  • Tongs, or spatula, or sharpened wood stick or storebought hot dog stick

  • Fork (optional)

Directions

  1. First, place the hot dogs on the grill or in the pan and let cook. If you cooking over a bonfire, then hold the stick over the fire, turning occasionally to cook fully.

  2. While you are waiting for the hot dogs to cook, get the buns out. Put in mayonnaise or any other condiments if you would like. If you are cooking with a bonfire, once the hot dog is cooked, before you follow the previous instructions, place the stick with the hot dog on it over a table to let it cool

  3. Place the hot dog on the bun and add any condiments that you would like on top.

  4. Put together anything else that you would like for side dishes.

  5. Enjoy!

Dinner Ideas

Rice, Meat, and Veggies

Lots of meals have little customizable options, and this is one of those meals. Only you can customize almost everything about it! You can pick the type of rice, type of meat, types of veggies, types of sauces, and more. To make it, you will need...

  • Any type of rice

  • Any type of meat

  • Any types of veggies

  • Any types of sauces

  • Pan spray

  • Frying pan or grill

  • Pot or rice cooker

  • Metal spatula or tongs

  • Plate or bowl

  • Fork

  • Cutting board and knife

  • Seasoning (optional)

Directions

  1. First, cook the rice. If you have a rice cooker, then put in the appropriate amount of water and follow the manufacturer's instructions.

  2. While the rice is cooking, cut and cook the meat.

  3. Then, cook the veggies.

  4. Combine everything together and season to taste.

  5. Enjoy!
