HTML Coding

Note: for this first "how to" subject, I can not simply put the code on the page, or the computer will think that it is part of the HTML coding that you can not see unless you inspect the page. So, if there is an asterick (*), then you do not need the dots that are in the code that I am showing to you.

For coding you need...

  • An html tag, like this: *<.html> <./html>

  • A head tag, like this: *<.head> <./head>

  • A body tag, like this: *<.body> <./body>

  • A title tag, like this: *<.title> <./title>

  • Different size tags, like these: *<.h1> <./h1>, *<.h2> <./h2>, etc. Note: this only goes up to six, and "h1" is the biggest, and "h6" is the smallest.

  • list tags, like the ones that I am using like this: *<.ul> <./ul>, *<.ol> <./ol>, *<.li> <./li>

There are many more tags, but these are the ones that you need to know to get started.

If you want to learn more of the details, then go here. They are only the first ten of the eighty-some videos that are on Myepassignments.com, a completely free homeschooling site.

Now, you can put these tags together like this...

Remember, don't type the dots in the tags!

<.html>

<.head>

<.title>My shopping list<./title>

<./head>

<.body>

<.h1>My shopping list<./h1>

<.ul>

<.li>item 1<./li>

<.li>item 2<./li>

<.li>item 3<./li>

<./ul>

<./html>

...And it'll look like this! (It just won't have any color except for black.)

  • item 2
  • item 3

Eggs and Toast

This simple yet delisious meal is easy to make. To make it, you will need...

  1. Eggs

  2. Toast

  3. Butter (optional)

  4. Salt and pepper (optional)

  5. Pan spray

  6. Frying pan

  7. Toaster

  8. Small bowl (optional)

  9. Metal spatula

  10. Plate

Directions

  • First, heat the pan on medium and spray with pan spray.

  • Then, crack the eggs on the side of the pan or the side of a smal bowl.

  • While the eggs are cooking, put some toast in the toaster and toast to your desire. Add butter if you would like.

  • When the whites aroun the eggs are solid, put salt and pepper on them if you would like, and then flip them.

  • When the eggs are done to your liking, use the metal spatula to pick the eggs off of the pan and on to a plate.

  • Sit down and enjoy!

Steak, Hashbrowns, and Eggs

This was the only picture that I could find that worked.

This meal is a little more work,but it is absolutely delicious. To make it, you will need...

  1. Steak

  2. Hashbrowns (you can use hashbrowns from a bag or make your own.)

  3. Eggs

  4. Butter (optional)

  5. Salt and pepper (optional)

  6. Pan spray

  7. Frying pan

  8. Toaster (it goes along with the toast.)

  9. Small bowl (optional)

  10. Metal spatula

  11. Plate

  12. Toast

Directions

  • First, heat the pan on medium and spray with pan spray.

  • Then, put the steak in the pan and cook to your desire. When the steak is done, use the metal spatula to take it off the pan and on to a plate.

  • Next, put the hashbrowns in the pan and cook to your desire.

  • While the hashbrowns are cooking, put some toast in the toaster and toast to desired texture. Add butter if you would like.

  • When the hashbrowns are done, use the metal spatula to take them off the pan and on to the plate.

  • Next, crack the eggs on the side of the pan or the side of a smal bowl.

  • When the whites aroun the eggs are solid, put salt and pepper on them if you would like, and then flip them.

  • When the eggs are done to your liking, use the metal spatula to pick the eggs off of the pan and on to the plate.

  • Sit down and enjoy!

Note: You can customize the ingredients and cooking times to suit your preferences in any of these recipes, and feel welcome to increase the amount of food being made if you are making this for more than one person.
